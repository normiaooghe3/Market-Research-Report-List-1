<p><p>The Garbanzo Flour market is primarily segmented into two types: Sprouted Bean Flour and Unsprouted Garbanzo Flour. Sprouted Bean Flour is made from beans that have been soaked and germinated, enhancing their nutritional profile and digestibility. It is often favored for its natural sweetness and flavor, making it popular in health-conscious recipes. Unsprouted Garbanzo Flour, on the other hand, is produced from dried chickpeas and retains a stronger, nuttier flavor. Both types cater to diverse culinary applications, from gluten-free baking to protein-rich dishes.</p></p>
