<p><p>Strain wave gearing devices, known for their high precision and compact design, are classified into three main types: cup style, hat style, and pancake style. </p><p>Cup style features a cup-shaped cam that allows flexibility while maintaining torque capacity. </p><p>Hat style incorporates a hat-like structure, providing robust gear reduction ratios suited for various applications. </p><p>Pancake style is characterized by its thin, flat profile, ideal for space-constrained environments. Each type offers unique advantages, catering to different industrial and robotics applications.</p></p>
