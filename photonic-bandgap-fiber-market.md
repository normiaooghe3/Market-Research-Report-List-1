<p><ul><li>Hollow Core</li><li>Solid Core</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The photonic bandgap fiber market is divided into two main types: hollow core and solid core fibers. Hollow core fibers utilize a central air-filled region that allows light to travel with minimal loss, enhancing efficiency and enabling ultra-fast communication. In contrast, solid core fibers have a solid silica core and are designed for high performance in traditional applications but may suffer from higher attenuation. Both types serve distinct applications, influencing bandwidth, transmission distance, and overall functionality in optical networks.</p></p>
